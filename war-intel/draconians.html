Draconians

Reptilian soldiers of the Shadow Queen. Unnatural, disciplined, and utterly devoted.

Creature Type
Description

Draconians are humanoid creatures with draconic features — scaled hides, clawed hands and feet, sharp teeth, and predatory eyes. They stand roughly human height, though often lean and muscular. Their bodies are built for combat, with an alien grace that is deeply unsettling.

Combat Capabilities
  • Exceptionally strong and fast
  • Armed with brutal, well-maintained weapons
  • Wear functional, military-grade armor
  • Possess natural claws and teeth as weapons
  • Show no fear or hesitation in combat
  • Fight with coordinated precision, following orders in unison
First Encounter

The party encountered draconians in the ambush on the road to Vogler. They moved with predatory grace, coordinating their attacks with military discipline. One was captured alive and questioned before it choked itself to death rather than provide further intelligence.

Intelligence & Speech
  • They speak Draconic fluently
  • They demonstrate clear tactical understanding and strategy
  • They follow commands instantly and without question
  • They show no mercy and no hesitation
  • When captured, they show no interest in negotiation or survival
Origin & Purpose

The draconians serve Takhisis, the Shadow Queen. Their creation is not natural — they are engineered soldiers, bred for war and absolute obedience. Their very existence is tied to her will.

They are not hired mercenaries or conscripted troops. They are believers. They speak of the Shadow Queen with reverence. Their loyalty is absolute and their discipline unwavering.

Tactical Implications

Draconians cannot be reasoned with or broken through morale collapse. They will fight to the death rather than surrender. They coordinate with inhuman efficiency, suggesting command structures and communication beyond simple sight and sound. Fighting them requires overwhelming force, clever tactics, or strategic advantage — not persuasion.

The Threat

A single draconian is a formidable opponent. Multiple draconians fighting in concert are devastating. The fact that they are being fielded in organized units across Ansalon — supporting the Red Dragon Army, pursuing refugees, occupying towns — suggests that Takhisis has access to an essentially unlimited supply of these creatures. This is not a war that can be won through attrition.
