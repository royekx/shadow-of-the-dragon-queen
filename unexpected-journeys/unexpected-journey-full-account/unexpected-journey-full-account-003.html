Unexpected Journey 003

The Beast Beneath Crustdurn Isle

Vogler welcomed them with warm meals and dark mysteries waiting in the river.

UJ 003 Party Level 2 Vogler

Vogler welcomed the travelers with the warmth of a town that knew how to mourn and celebrate in the same breath. Children ran through festival preparations, villagers spoke with easy familiarity, and the Brass Crab offered food, drink, and stories. Roshi sought out crab ramen despite the obvious danger to her health, turning the meal into an act of stubborn devotion to memory and appetite. Everywhere, people spoke fondly of Ispin Greenshield, recounting impossible tales of goblins, owlbears, mountains, and narrow escapes, each story half legend and half truth.

Aratos visited Gertrude's smithy and met Becklin, the retired Knight of Solamnia who had known Ispin well. He told her of the vision that had set him on the road and made clear that he meant to warn the knights of what might be coming. Becklin did not dismiss him, though there was little she could immediately do from Vogler. Still, Aratos found in her someone grounded enough to hear the fear in his warning and respected enough to help carry it onward.

At the docks, Mayriel's day shifted sharply. Near a fish statue touched by locals for luck, she was struck by another vision. She saw a battlefield beneath the moons, reptilian soldiers marching against knights, and above them a horned woman in dark regalia commanding slaughter. That figure spoke against the followers of Paladine, then unfolded into the terrible shape of a five-headed dragon. Flames of many colors descended, only for platinum fire to rise in answer and shield Mayriel from annihilation. When the vision ended, Fizban found her shaken and reminded her that if the gods were speaking, she should listen.

Aratos also found family in Vogler. His cousin Aeimos appeared with familiar Stormblast markings and the easy audacity to steal Aratos's ring as a prank before revealing himself. Their reunion carried the warmth of old bonds and the roughness of shared blood. Aratos told Aeimos of his vision and the danger ahead, asking him to come toward Kalaman and the Knights of Solamnia. Aeimos agreed, bringing another thread of Aratos's past into the present danger.

As the group compared what they had learned, the strange sounds near the river became impossible to ignore. Fishermen reported poor catches, the statue bore divine traces, and a lonely island in the river drew their attention. Kakashi sent his raven to scout the abandoned shack there, and the party rented a rowboat to reach Crustdurn Isle. The island was rumored to be haunted, avoided by locals, and marked by the sort of silence that made curiosity feel like trespass.

Inside the abandoned shack, the party discovered signs of something below. A hatch led them down into a cavern beneath the island, where the air was damp, the ground littered with fish bones, and the sound of a creature echoed through the dark. Old writings suggested that something beneath the island had once needed to be fed or appeased. The party pressed onward, and the creature woke.

The monster that rose against them was a grotesque thing of frog-like mass and grasping tentacles. Kakashi struck first with a burst of ice, Jasper disappeared into shadows and punished openings, Aratos and Aeimos brought steel and lightning, Mayriel channeled divine harm, and Roshi charged into danger. The creature swallowed Roshi, turning the battle into a frantic effort to kill it before it could consume her. Aratos drove lightning through its body, Jasper's arrows found killing angles, and Aeimos leapt into the fray with storm-charged fists. Together, the Stormblast cousins and their companions brought the creature down.

Within the cavern, they recovered treasure from whatever had been dragged or offered to the beast, including a moon-touched sword and boots marked by strange magic. Aeimos cut out the creature's tongue in grim trophy, and the party returned to the mainland with proof that the danger beneath Crustdurn Isle had been real. Becklin met them with gratitude and surprise, especially at the monstrous remains they carried back. The town had lost fish and lived beside a sleeping threat without knowing how close it had come to waking fully. For one more day, at least, Vogler was safe.
