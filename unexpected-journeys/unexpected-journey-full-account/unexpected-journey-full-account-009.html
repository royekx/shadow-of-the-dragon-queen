Unexpected Journey 009

The Reliquary of Lost Oaths

Beneath a hidden door, knights make their stand against corruption itself.

UJ 009 Party Level 4 Outside Kalaman

Evening settled over Kalaman as the refugees of Vogler gathered outside the city walls, their tents pitched beneath the shadow of a place that had offered shelter, but not yet belonging. The party returned to Mayor Raven with the council's answer: the people of Vogler would be allowed refuge, though the aid came bound to expectation. Kalaman had troubles of its own, and the party had agreed to lend their strength to the city's defense.

Raven received the news with weary gratitude. She told them that most of the villagers had been settled as best they could, with tents and fresh water enough for the moment. Food, however, remained a question. Still, she thanked them again and named them honorary Voglerans, a small honor from a people who had lost almost everything except one another. The party also warned her that Lord Bakaris had already forced his way into Kalaman's politics, and Raven's reaction made clear that she had expected nothing better from him.

It was then that Fizban, ever strange and seemingly untouched by ordinary urgency, suggested a remedy for despair: stew. He claimed to know one of Ispin's old recipes, a hearty dish that might fill empty stomachs and lift wounded spirits. What he needed was meat. Bear, deer, hare, anything the wilds might offer. The party, needing something simple after days of fire and grief, set out to hunt.

Outside Kalaman, the hunt quickly became less simple than intended. Aeimos had returned, but true to his nature, he did not walk openly into the group. He lingered at the edges, moving unseen through the camp and following the others at a distance. Jasper noticed him first, the quiet recognition passing between two people who understood the value of remaining unseen. Aeimos, pipe in hand and senses sharpened by the strange smoke he drew into his lungs, watched the party move into the wild.

The group split their attention between bear and deer. Roshi, Aratos, Jasper, and Kakashi followed signs of a bear, finding tracks, broken branches, and the places where the animal had passed. Mayariel pursued deer instead, only to find herself separated from the others. Aeimos saw the deer before she did and tried to approach silently to point them out, but his altered senses betrayed him. He stepped too loudly, and Mayariel whirled on him. The moment might have been awkward enough on its own, but then Aeimos saw something behind her: a pale, beautiful woman with pointed ears and long flowing hair, watching him with unsettling calm. She pointed toward the direction the others had gone, then vanished.

Aeimos could not tell exactly what she was, but he understood the warning. Something was wrong. He and Mayariel hurried to the others, where the bear trail had led to a cave. Jasper had already scouted near its mouth and found no bear inside, only a faint blue glow pulsing from deeper within. Mayariel, looking more closely, noticed purplish-black soot on the floor, a sign that stirred memory of the ashen horrors the party had fought on the river. What had seemed like a hunt now felt like a trail laid by something darker.

They entered cautiously, with Aeimos and Roshi near the front and the others close behind. The cave opened onto a heavy stone door marked by the symbol of the Knights Radiant, but altered. The familiar sword bore not four points, but six. When the party approached, symbols lit around the door, and the air carried a silent demand. It wanted something from them. Not a key, not strength alone, but oath.

Aratos stepped forward first. He felt warmth and storm rise inside him, the weight of his path pressing against his chest. He whispered the words that shaped him: life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination. One spoke of the symbol lit, and the word Valor appeared.

Aeimos followed, shaken by the weight of the moment and by the path that had led him there. He spoke his own truth beneath his breath: hopelessness is not my privilege. Another spoke lit, and the word Resolve appeared. Tears came with it, not from weakness, but from the force of feeling something ancient recognize the oath inside him.

As the party pieced together the nature of the door, Kakashi understood that the symbols were not asking for one person's vow alone. They were seeking the virtues of knighthood. The door tested what each person carried. Knowledge stirred beneath his understanding. Roshi touched the door and offered the truth that drove her: to strive for the best and for the greater good. Another spoke answered with Foresight. Mayariel, reflecting on her own oath to leave the world better than she found it, felt her resolve resonate with the same deeper duty of protection. At last, the door opened.

Beyond lay a hidden reliquary. Its passage was lined with murals telling a forgotten story: Knights of Solamnia and Knights Radiant standing together beneath a floating city, then those same forces diminished as the city fell burning from the sky. One knight in particular appeared with a horn, leading or rallying others. Someone had recently defaced part of the mural, smearing it with the same purplish-black ash that marked the cave. Whatever had entered this place had not come as a pilgrim.

Aeimos felt again the strange pull from the pale woman's warning. A whisper urged him forward, and he chose a path that led down a broken staircase. He descended with almost impossible grace, running along walls and broken stone while the others followed with far less elegance. Roshi struggled and had to be helped down. Kakashi tumbled the rest of the way, landing hard at the bottom. But below, the clanking of metal and the sound of chanting drew all attention forward.

The chamber beneath was a tomb of knights. Armored dead stood in place along the walls, their bodies preserved as memorials. In the center stood a knight holding a horn in the crook of his arm, the same figure from the murals. Before him, a withered necromantic figure chanted over a spectral black rose with two petals. Two of the knightly dead had already risen from their places and walked with swords in hand.

The party struck quickly. Kakashi unleashed Witch Bolt, lightning crashing down from above and wracking the chanting figure, but the necromancer did not stop. Jasper glided down with the aid of his boots and fired from the shadows. Aeimos saw the connection between the necromancer, the black rose, and the central knight, understanding that the ritual was feeding power into the tomb's honored dead. He activated his storm-charged gauntlets and struck one of the risen knights with lightning from a distance, battering it down before it could close.

Aratos descended into the chamber and called upon his ancestors. Fire and storm rose around him as he drew the Emberwake Greataxe, and spectral figures appeared at his side: a towering bald warrior, a kingly hooded figure, and a slim blue-hued guardian with a spear. He charged the necromancer, his oath and rage moving together, and drove his weapon into the undead caster. Mayariel followed with a charge of her own, her greatsword biting into the creature before divine power erupted from her blade in the form of radiant rose-thorns. The image of her light against the necromancer's black rose made the chamber feel like a battlefield of symbols as much as steel.

The necromancer answered with darkness. A black-purple orb spread through the chamber, swallowing the party in magical night. Inside it, whispers and wet, monstrous noises filled the air, and cold necrotic power gnawed at those trapped within. Kakashi maintained his lightning despite the pain, striking the necromancer again through the darkness. Jasper slipped free of the shadow and landed a shot that finally broke the necromancer's concentration, causing the orb to vanish.

But the ritual had already gone far enough.

As Aeimos pummeled the necromancer into the ground, the creature gasped final words in service to Lord Soth. The rose's dark power surged into the central knight, and the honored dead rose as a wraith. The ritual had not been completed, but it had succeeded enough to corrupt what lay in the reliquary. The black rose dissolved, and the knight's spirit emerged in violet shadow, armored and terrible.

The wraith's presence rolled through the room like despair made manifest. Fear gripped most of the party, rooting them in place and making every movement toward it feel impossible. Aeimos studied the chamber and understood what the incomplete ritual meant: the wraith was dangerous, but not as powerful as it might have become. Radiant power would wound it most deeply. He called that knowledge to the others.

Mayariel answered. Her sacred flame burned against the wraith, and Roshi used divine power to turn the undead, forcing the lesser risen knights to recoil from her presence. Aratos hacked down one of the skeletal knights, while Jasper fought through fear and pain, wounded by the wraith's necrotic touch until his very life force felt diminished. Kakashi surged forward with lightning screaming like a thousand birds as he drove it into the wraith and forced the Boilerdrake Heart beyond its limits, adding fire to the strike. Aeimos closed with storm-lit fists, his oath hardening against the wraith's promise that his light would fade.

At last, Roshi's spiritual weapon became the killing blow. It took the shape of a rose, its petals unfolding into dagger-like blades before closing around the wraith like a divine snare. The shadow was snuffed out. The corrupted spirit gave way to something nobler: the knight as he had been, solemn and confused, bearing a spectral shield and horn.

He thanked them for their bravery and for freeing the tomb from the evil that had taken hold. To Aratos and Aeimos, he named them children of Cathan and told them they had proven themselves. Then he raised his horn and blew. The spirits of the knights who had been twisted into undeath rose from their fallen forms and returned to their places of rest. The chamber grew still again, no longer pulsing with purple corruption, but quiet with reverence.

In the aftermath, the party searched what remained. The necromancer's body yielded an intricate armor of bone, heavy with necrotic energy, and a handful of gold. The horn from the central statue drew the Stormblast cousins' attention. Aeimos lifted it and blew, though no sound came to mortal ears. Instead, two spectral knights appeared, armored and waiting for command. The horn was no simple relic; it could call aid from the honored dead. In the chamber, the name of the knight remained: Sir Cthondor.

Before leaving, Aeimos bowed in respect. This was the first true reliquary of the Knights Radiant he had ever known, and its discovery carried a weight beyond treasure. The party emerged from the hidden place and, almost absurdly, returned to the hunt they had set out on. They found a bear after all and brought it back to camp.

Fizban, seemingly unaware of the battle beneath the hill, greeted the bear as though the party had simply taken longer than expected. With help from the Vogler refugees, the meat was prepared, and Ispin's stew came together over the fire. Whether the recipe was truly Ispin's or simply another of Fizban's wandering claims mattered little. The people ate, and for one night at least, warmth and food softened the edges of loss. Darrett eventually joined them, covered in sweat and dirt from training, and the camp shared what comfort it could.

As the evening settled, Aeimos left the horn with Aratos and slipped away again, as was his way. Aratos looked for him but found only absence and the sense that his cousin had gone where he needed to go. Jasper, meanwhile, found a note written in Thieves' Cant, marked with the symbol of the Dragon's Eye. The message was simple and dangerous: We need to talk.

Beyond the camp, Aeimos saw the pale woman once more. This time he approached her and asked for guidance. She told him that Lord Soth sought to corrupt all knights: their image, their legacies, and the memory of what they had been. He would continue until his curse was lifted. When Aeimos asked where to go, she pointed into the distance. Then she vanished.

The campfires of Kalaman burned behind him. Somewhere ahead, there were more tombs, more knights, more legacies in danger of being twisted by Soth's vengeance. Aeimos understood that Aratos had his path, and he had his own. For now, both roads led deeper into the same shadow.
