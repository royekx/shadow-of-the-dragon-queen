Unexpected Journey 007

Flames Over Vogler

The sky darkens with wings. A dragon comes to Krynn.

UJ 007 Party Level 3 Vogler Docks

The last boats of Vogler strained against the docks as smoke rolled across the river. Behind the fleeing villagers, the town that had welcomed the party in Ispin's name was coming apart beneath fire, steel, and the roar of the Red Dragon Army. The evacuation had nearly succeeded when the sky darkened with wings. Someone looked up and cried the word that had not been spoken in Krynn with certainty for centuries: dragon.

The creature swept over the docks with its mouth full of flame. Below it, the last defenders and draconians still clashed near the waterfront, buying whatever seconds they could for the villagers to escape. Then the dragon breathed. Fire poured across the ground, and those still caught in its path were reduced to ash. The sight of it froze the moment in horror. This was not rumor, not vision, not old legend remembered wrong. A dragon had come to Vogler.

From Thornwall Keep, Than Rookledust found his own impossible path to the battle. Trapped on the roof as flames climbed the tower, he launched himself from the gnome-flinger and sailed through the air toward the docks. By luck, skill, or the stubborn confidence of a gnome who had done this before, he landed on his feet and saw more than most had time to notice. The dragon was not alone. Smaller winged shapes moved through the smoke, and a humanoid figure rode the great beast before leaping down toward the battlefield.

Mayriel answered the terror with light. She lifted her holy symbol, and a twilight radiance spread from her, wrapping her companions in a dim, protective glow. In that small sphere of calm amid burning streets, the party found just enough courage to stand. Jasper took to the air with the winged boots, rising above the docks and loosing an arrow at the dragon. His first shot faltered under the impossible sight of the creature, but he forced his fear into focus and struck true. Roshi raised her bow as well, though the moving beast was difficult to mark through smoke and flame. Kakashi blurred himself with magic and slipped toward cover, preparing for whatever came next.

Then the rider landed. Green-skinned and orcish, carrying a flaming axe, the warrior looked across the party and mocked what he had heard of Vogler's heroes. He named himself Theros Flametongue and met Aratos like a man eager for a worthy kill. Aratos charged him without hesitation, rage rising as ancestral silhouettes gathered around him: large, brutal guardians and swift, hooded figures circling in silence. His axe bit into Theros, and the enemy laughed as if the wound were an invitation.

The duel became the heart of the burning dock. Theros struck back with fire and steel, forcing Aratos to hold the line while the dragon's body glowed hotter above them. Flame gathered first in its belly, then climbed toward its throat, then burst outward in another sweeping cone. The party scattered as best they could, some finding shelter, others simply enduring the blast. Than Rookledust, running through the chaos, found one of the red draconians and shot it cleanly, only for the creature's body to convulse and explode in death. Even the fallen enemies were dangerous.

Mayriel blessed her companions, golden draconic light settling over them as the battle grew more desperate. Jasper, still above the field, watched the dragon carefully and realized the truth hidden beneath the fear. It did not move like flesh. Its body shuddered with mechanisms, smoke, and metal. It was not a true dragon at all, but a terrible machine, crewed by figures seated within its frame. With that knowledge, the fight changed. Arrows and spells began seeking seams, seats, and weak points rather than merely scales.

Kakashi surged forward with lightning in his hand, the sound of a thousand birds screaming through the smoke as he struck Theros with crackling force. Roshi's arrows found the dragon. Jasper kept firing from above, harrying the machine and its operators. Mayriel conjured a spiritual blade that hovered near her, striking as she fought. Theros, battered but defiant, refused to fall. He knocked weapons aside, rushed through the line, and tried to break Mayriel's stance, forcing the party to divide their attention between the axe-wielding warrior and the fire-breathing engine overhead.

At last, Mayriel brought Theros low. She reached out with necrotic force, driving him to one knee as he snarled that he would not die to nothings. Then her spiritual sword drove into his throat. Theros collapsed, purple blood spilling across the ground. For a moment, it seemed one great threat had ended.

The dragon-machine still burned above them. Aratos, hearing again the cold voice that had tested him before, hurled his javelin into the sky. Lightning tore through the construct. The strike ripped into its mechanisms, and screams rang out from within as explosions burst across its frame. The dragon's tail spun down, its body failed, and the great machine crashed into the docks in a storm of fire, metal, and ruin. The blast threw the battlefield into chaos. Some held their feet. Others fell. Roshi went down, preserved from death only by the magic she carried.

The wreckage still held one living operator, half-buried in the ruined mouth of the machine. Mayriel saw him moving. Jasper fired but could not quite finish him. Kakashi walked into the wreckage, reached into the broken remains, and released another surge of lightning. The final operator died screaming, and his death triggered another explosion that dropped Kakashi as well. As the fire cleared, memories flashed through him: a village burning, people crying, and a past he had tried to bury beneath distance and silence.

The party staggered through the aftermath, wounded and exhausted but alive. Than Rookledust rushed between the fallen, feeding potions to those who needed them. Mayriel healed Aratos, and then, feeling a divine pull deepen in her chest, offered Paladine something precious: her family signet ring and a promise of obedience when the god would one day ask something of her. The ring glowed with holy light, and the golden canary that had appeared to her before swept into view. Its radiance expanded, washing over the party and restoring them just enough to move.

Jasper looked back toward the burning town and saw more figures moving through the smoke. Jeyev appeared with Darrett on his back, carrying the young squire away from the front lines. Finbar, unseen by most, emerged as well, making for the boat with the same practical instinct that had once nearly carried him away from Vogler before the worst began. The party gathered what they could. Kakashi pulled a hot glowing gem from the dragon wreckage, realizing it had been the source of the machine's fire. Aratos took Theros's still-warm axe. There was no time for more.

They reached the boat as Vogler burned behind them. The river carried them away from the docks, but none could look away. Through the smoke, they saw Becklin Uth Viharin and Cudgel Ironsmile still fighting near the waterfront, holding back a taller woman with charred skin, blackened armor, and a massive sword. Cudgel was kicked aside through a building. Becklin fought on, parrying and pushing through draconians until she reached the edge of the dock. She looked out toward the departing boat, resigned to her fate and grateful that the others had escaped.

Roshi loosed an arrow toward the enemy, but the distance and motion carried it wide. Becklin raised her hands as if to say it was all right. Then blades struck her from behind, and she fell into the dark water.

As the boat drifted farther down the Vingaard River, other figures emerged from the smoke. One stood over the fallen body of Theros Flametongue, and flame passed across him until he rose again. Another voice called after the fleeing survivors: run as far as you can. You will not escape the flames of the Dragon Queen.

Vogler disappeared behind smoke, fire, and ash. The party had saved many lives, but the town that had given them shelter, stories, food, and Ispin's memory was lost behind them. They had survived the battle, but survival did not feel like victory. It felt like the first breath after drowning, with the river carrying them toward an uncertain road and the Dragon Queen's fire burning behind them.
