Unexpected Journey 005

Shadows in the Woods

An army of five hundred waits beyond Vogler, and time slips like water.

UJ 005 Party Level 3 Vogler Surrounds

The aftermath of High Hill was quiet in the way only a battlefield can be quiet. Bodies lay scattered across the hill, militia and mercenary alike, while Becklin gave orders and survivors were carried back toward town. Cudgel's fury burned hot. One of the traitorous mercenaries had been kept alive, and she wanted answers from him with the kind of anger that barely left room for restraint. Becklin, though furious herself, kept her composure. Anger, she made clear, could not be allowed to make the next decision for them.

The walk back into Vogler was grim. Festival banners still hung across the town, cheerful and bright, while bloodied survivors were brought beneath them. Villagers who had not seen the battle came out pale-faced and horrified, then immediately began helping where they could. The contrast was cruel: only hours before, Vogler had been celebrating its history; now it was living through the beginning of another.

At the Brass Crab, the captured mercenary was awakened and questioned. Cudgel nearly lost herself to rage, shaking and striking him, but Raven stepped in before the interrogation became nothing but vengeance. Aratos leaned close and made the threat clear in a quieter, more dangerous way. The man finally broke. He claimed he did not know everything, only that Cudgel's lieutenant, Gragonis, had gone into the woods and returned with gold. Some of the mercenaries had been paid to betray Cudgel, slaughter the militia, and leave Vogler vulnerable. The coin had been good enough, and for men with weak loyalty, that was enough.

The name struck Cudgel hard. Gragonis had been her lieutenant, one of her own. His betrayal was not only tactical; it was personal. The prisoner said the surviving traitors had likely retreated toward the mercenary camp, while something larger waited beyond the woods. Cudgel insisted on going back to her camp herself to see who remained loyal and who had turned. The party considered sending someone with her, fearing a trap, but she refused a direct escort. Her people were her responsibility.

Kakashi sent his raven to follow Cudgel at a distance, while Aratos, Kakashi, Jasper, and Darrett set out to scout the woods where the traitorous lieutenant had gone. Roshi and Mayriel remained in Vogler to help the wounded and organize whatever defenses the town could muster. The group split not out of comfort, but necessity. Vogler needed eyes beyond its walls and hands inside them.

Before leaving, Aratos was struck by a vision. He saw impossible skies and a colossal tree split between flame and frost. At its base stood a stern, ancient figure who presented him with a choice: blades bound in fury and vengeance, or an axe marked by cold restraint and resolve. The words pressed into him like a command: choose, boy. The gods are watching. When Aratos woke, the chill of the vision remained, and with it the sense that his strength was being weighed by something far older than himself.

The scouting party followed Darrett into the woods, though the young squire's confidence occasionally outpaced his sense of direction. After hours of careful travel, they reached the edge of an encampment. Jasper moved ahead alone, vanishing into the dark as only he could. What he found was not a handful of mercenaries, but a true military presence: one to two hundred armed figures, organized, supplied, and unafraid. They were not nervous raiders. They were soldiers waiting in strength.

Jasper also noticed patrols that included a larger, more bestial figure resembling the creatures the party had fought on the road. When a patrol drew too close, he tried to distract them with a thrown rock and nearly gave himself away. Thinking quickly, he used the Dragon's Eye coin to disguise himself as one of the guards and pretended to be relieving himself against a tree. The deception was crude, ridiculous, and just convincing enough to keep him alive. Aratos, watching from a distance, caught sight of one of the larger figures and recognized the same ominous shape from earlier battles.

Back in Vogler, Roshi and Mayriel did what they could among the wounded. Roshi laid hands on an injured fisherman, and divine warmth passed through her into him. He looked at her with awe, asking whether she had been touched by the gods. The question lingered because both of them knew what it meant: such things had not been seen in living memory. Later, Roshi reflected on Ispin, on the way he had carved himself into the hearts of others, and on whether she had done the same. With Ispin's shield near her, his presence felt close, not as a ghost, but as a memory with weight.

Mayriel followed a small bird through the wounded and found a woman hiding a fatal injury while insisting others needed care more than she did. Mayriel saw through the brave face and healed her with golden divine light. The woman wept in gratitude, and once again the old question emerged: had Mayriel been touched by the gods? In a town filled with blood and fear, the return of divine mercy was no small thing.

When the scouts returned, they told Mayor Raven, Becklin, and the others what they had seen. The army beyond Vogler was too large to dismiss and too organized to ignore. The town began strengthening what defenses it could, setting watches, and preparing for the possibility that the Red Army would come sooner rather than later. During a patrol of the town, Jasper found Old Man Jenkins packing to flee. Jenkins had no intention of dying for Vogler, but Jasper's decision to stay surprised him. When Jasper said the town was good and its people needed help, Jenkins grudgingly gave him aid: two healing potions and special bolts meant to leech life from their targets. Then he prepared to vanish before morning.

By the end of the night, Vogler had little certainty but much to fear. Cudgel had not yet returned. The Red Army camp was real. The town's walls were weak, its people wounded, and its defenders few. Still, the party chose to remain.
