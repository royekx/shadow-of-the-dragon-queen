Unexpected Journey 004

Tales, Tears, and Treachery

Ispin's memory became a shield, and Vogler became a battlefield.

← Previous Brief Account Next →
UJ 004 Party Level 2 Vogler

As sunset came to Vogler, the town gathered by the river to say farewell to Ispin Greenshield. His body lay wrapped in a shroud inside a small boat, resting upon the water of the Vingaard River. Becklin stood before the mourners in her Solamnic armor and spoke with gentle strength. Ispin had asked for tales, not tears, and so the town gave him what he wanted. Becklin and Cudgel lowered the boat into the river, and as it drifted into the fading light, the people watched until the vessel sank from sight.

The quiet of the river gave way to the roar of the Brass Crab. Inside, grief became laughter, tankards clashed, food was passed freely, and stories of Ispin rose from every corner of the room. Some swore he had ridden down a volcano on his shield. Others claimed he had leapt from a mountain into shallow water and survived. Fizban told a tale of a stolen pie and an offended prince. Becklin remembered a so-called little adventure against a wyrmling that ended with her and Ispin laughing their way out of disaster.

Not every voice honored the dead kindly. Bakaris the Younger, a blond nobleman with more arrogance than grace, interrupted the stories and mocked the affection people held for Ispin. Becklin's patience ended quickly. She told him that if he had nothing fond to say, he should retire for the evening. Bakaris left in a huff, but not before making clear that he thought himself and his father above the town's simple grief.

Roshi added a story of Ispin and a bear den, telling how he had once gone after honey and waited for the bears to wake so he could compliment them on it. The tale ended with Roshi wrestling the mother bear to save him, and the absurdity of it fit perfectly among the other impossible memories. Then Kakashi spoke more solemnly. He described Ispin as a man guided by curiosity rather than fear, someone who treated life as an invitation to explore, ask questions, change, and find joy in the unknown. As he spoke, the room quieted. When he finished, the people raised their drinks to Ispin, and Becklin thanked him for the words.

The night also gave the party a clearer shape. Becklin introduced herself properly, the travelers gave their names, and the question of what came next hung over them. Fizban said he had to leave for Kalaman in the morning, while Aratos pressed the need to warn the knights. Mayriel weighed her own vision and the danger it implied. The group was still not fully a party, not in the clean and easy sense, but they had fought together, mourned together, and begun to choose the same road.

Becklin revealed that Ispin had intended his green shield to pass on, though Fizban insisted it belonged with someone still willing to stand in front of danger. Roshi accepted the shield, to receive it properly the next morning at Thornwall Keep. Mayor Raven offered the group lodging in her home, extending Vogler's hospitality even as unease gathered beneath the festival cheer.

Jasper's night took a secret turn. A note appeared in his pocket instructing him to meet at General Goods and come alone. He slipped out unseen and found Old Man Jenkins, whose appearance hid something stranger beneath. The old man was connected to the Dragon's Eye and knew the dangerous phrase Jasper had been taught to remember: none shall hide from the Dragon's Eye. Jenkins explained more about the guild, Lucky, the deadly coin, and the impossible task of reaching the moving Tower of Wayreth to steal the Orb of Dragonkind. He also sold Jasper a pair of winged boots, which Jasper purchased with the party's shared gold and later defended as a "slick deal."

Meanwhile, Aratos and Mayriel shared their visions with Becklin. They described the reptilian creatures, the Shadow Queen, and the sense that an attack was coming. Becklin could not explain the creatures, but she believed their concern was genuine and promised to send them with a letter that might help them gain an audience in Kalaman. Her word as a retired knight might not open every door, but it would be more than nothing.

Morning brought parting gifts and new burdens. Fizban left early and took Aeimos along with him, leaving Kakashi a spell as a farewell gift. Mayriel woke to a golden finch and a pendant meant to aid her divine path. Darrett, Becklin's young squire, arrived to bring the party to Thornwall Keep. There, Becklin gave Roshi Ispin's Green Shield. When Roshi took it, it felt as though the shield had come home, and the memory of Ispin settled into her hands.

The party agreed to take part in the Kingfisher Festival's mock battle, a reenactment of Vogler's founding conflict. Before the event, they crossed paths again with Bakaris the Younger and found him just as insufferable as before. Aratos and Kakashi tried to draw useful information from him about Kalaman and his father, but it became clear the young noble had very little true authority. He only wore arrogance like armor.

At High Hill, the festival reached its martial heart. Mayor Raven led the parade, Darrett stood among the militia, and Cudgel's mercenaries took the role of the old Istarian foe. At first, it was all pageantry. Darrett called the charge, Aratos moved to support him, Roshi strode into the fray, Mayriel joined the fighting with awkward enthusiasm, Jasper moved freely through the chaos, and Kakashi took to the air with the winged boots. Then one of the mercenaries struck with an unpadded blade, and a villager screamed.

The mock battle became a massacre. Jasper reacted instantly, killing a traitorous mercenary before Darrett could fall. Aratos shouted for the militia to protect themselves and stayed near Darrett. Roshi stood against the attackers with Ispin's shield and moon-touched steel. Mayriel's divine magic flashed, sometimes wild, sometimes devastating. Kakashi struggled to fight from the air but kept pressing his magic into the battle. More enemies rode in, and with them came a huge half-ogre.

The ogre nearly crushed Roshi, driving her close to collapse, but she answered with divine fire. Her blade erupted with searing power and tore into the creature, its strange blood splattering across her as she refused to give ground. Around her, the others cut down attackers one by one. Darrett found his courage and struck true. Jasper's arrows ended enemies before they could recover. Aratos held the line, and Mayriel's light burned through the chaos.

At last, Jasper shot the ogre through the head, and Roshi ducked behind Ispin's shield as the massive body fell. Becklin and Cudgel entered the field, cutting through the remaining attackers and pushing the ambush into retreat. Cudgel was horrified to find her own mercenaries had betrayed her. On the ogre, the party found a note: "For your services from the Red Army." The celebration had ended. Vogler was no longer simply a town in mourning. It was a town at war.
