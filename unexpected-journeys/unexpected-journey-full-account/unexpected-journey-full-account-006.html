Unexpected Journey 006

The Dragon Queen's Demand

An ultimatum in red and gold, delivered at sunset. Surrender or burn.

UJ 006 Party Level 3 Vogler

Morning came with little rest for Vogler's leaders. Mayor Raven looked exhausted, pacing through her home with the weight of every villager on her shoulders. At the Brass Crab, Becklin and Darrett studied a map of the town, marking places that could be reinforced if the army came. Then Kakashi's raven returned to the window, pecking for entry with news from Cudgel. It reported that Cudgel had found her mercenary camp, that some had been captured, and that she was returning soon.

Cudgel arrived with a shaved-headed man named Jeyev, one of her loyal lieutenants. Her report confirmed the worst. The army encampment was real, positioned to the northwest, and the remaining loyal mercenaries had survived the betrayal. Some traitors had been captured, but the danger was far larger than a broken mercenary company. Cudgel laid out the hard truth before Becklin, Raven, and the party: Vogler could stay and fight, or it could evacuate.

Mayor Raven resisted the thought of abandoning the town. Vogler was not merely buildings and docks; it was home. But the party pressed the need for a plan beyond pride. Becklin quietly agreed that escape had to be prepared, even if it was a last resort. The river and boats offered a possible way out, though the vessels would need repairs and the evacuation of hundreds would be chaotic. Cudgel offered to send word to her remaining mercenaries so they could help hold the northern approach. Kakashi considered sending his raven toward Kalaman, though that would take time they did not have.

Lord Bakaris the Elder then forced his way into the discussion, dressed in red and gold and offended that anyone had dared make plans without him. Becklin, Raven, and Cudgel reacted with immediate irritation, but Kakashi tried to turn Bakaris's vanity into usefulness. He praised the lord as exactly the sort of authority needed to carry word to Kalaman. Bakaris, predictably, preferred sending a letter with his seal rather than riding himself into danger. Even so, the idea of getting word to Kalaman remained part of the plan.

Then a villager burst in with worse news. A rider in black and red armor waited at the gate, asking for whoever spoke for Vogler. Raven, Becklin, and Cudgel went to meet him, while Roshi broke away toward Thornwall Keep in search of anything that might help fortify the town. At the gate, the rider handed Mayor Raven a scroll and remained mounted in silence.

The message was a command from the Red Dragon Army in the name of the Dragon Queen. Vogler was to open its doors by sunset, quarter the soldiers beneath its roofs, feed them, shelter them, and comply. Refusal would bring swift and final mercy. It was not a negotiation. It was judgment.

While the messenger waited below, Roshi met Than, a frantic gnomish tinkerer at Thornwall Keep. He was strange, scattered, and difficult to keep focused, but useful. Roshi asked for help delaying a massacre, and Than admitted he had unstable explosives that might be set as traps. He could also help repair boats and perhaps rig some kind of signal, though nothing powerful enough to reliably reach Kalaman without risking disaster. From the keep, Roshi spotted several figures on the cliff above the gate wearing armor like the messenger's. The rider had not come alone.

Mayor Raven gathered the people of Vogler and told them the truth. The town had been ordered to surrender itself to an unknown army by sunset or face slaughter. Panic broke out almost immediately. Some demanded answers, others begged to flee, and Bakaris stirred fear from the crowd's edge. Kakashi stepped forward, struck his staff, and commanded silence with a flash of power. For a moment the sky itself seemed to darken, and the crowd stilled. He told them there was a plan, that the mayor and defenders were working together, and that people needed to begin packing, loading boats, and preparing to move. His force gave way to gentleness, and that balance helped turn panic into motion. Villagers began offering boats, labor, and aid.

The party then turned to buying time. Cudgel needed to get Jeyev out to warn and gather her loyal mercenaries, but the messenger and cliffside watchers could not be allowed to notice. A plan formed around distraction and misdirection. Roshi and Kakashi would occupy the messenger below. Aratos, Mayriel, and Jasper would deal with the watchers above. Jeyev would ride when an opening appeared.

The cliffside plan fell apart almost immediately, though not without style. Jasper vanished into position, but Aratos and Mayriel's climb was loud enough to draw attention. Aratos tried to bluff one of the watchers and then shove him from the cliff, but the attempt failed, and a blade came to his throat. Below, Kakashi created the sound of someone screaming for help, and Roshi ran toward it to sell the illusion. The distraction was good enough for Jeyev to spur his horse and begin his escape, but the enemy reacted quickly, and battle broke out on both fronts.

On the cliff, Mayriel called down a moonlit beam of divine power that burned one of the enemies in radiant light. Jasper struck from hiding and kept repositioning. Aratos raged and fought through burning blades, though his axe grew cold in his hand and a voice demanded he prove himself. Cudgel eventually climbed into the fight and joined them, arriving in time to see one enemy reduced to ash by Mayriel's moonlight. The battle was dangerous; Mayriel was struck hard and fell unconscious, hovering close to death before she was brought back by a healing potion.

Below, Roshi challenged the mounted messenger and drew his attention, absorbing blows that might otherwise have gone toward Jeyev. Kakashi armored himself in magic and pressed forward. The messenger tried to intercept Jeyev, but the party's distractions and attacks slowed him long enough for the rider to keep moving. Aratos eventually lined up his javelin and hurled it with brutal precision. It struck the messenger through the heart, killing him and sending his horse fleeing.

The cliffside enemies fell as well. Kakashi unleashed a crackling lightning strike that sounded like a thousand birds and made him seem, for a brief instant, younger and sharper, as if the spell had pulled an older strength through him. Jasper used one of Jenkins's leeching arrows to strike a surviving foe. Cudgel crushed another. When the last enemies were dead, Jeyev was gone into the distance with his message, and the party had bought Vogler a little more time.

Among the fallen, they found two healing potions, a magical amulet, a blue-gemmed ring, and gold. The amulet was later identified as a periapt that could preserve someone from death when they fell, and the ring offered protective magic to whoever wore it. These were not treasures won from adventure for adventure's sake; they were tools claimed in the shadow of an approaching army.

As afternoon slipped toward evening, Vogler moved with desperate purpose. Townsfolk repaired boats, carried supplies, helped Than prepare unstable traps, and readied themselves for flight. Cudgel's mercenaries established a position north of town to slow the Red Army. Becklin called the party together and gave them their charge. She and Cudgel would stand at the front line. The party would help the villagers escape and serve as the last line if the defenders broke.

Before she left, Becklin made one personal request of Aratos. There was a chest in Thornwall Keep, and if she did not return, he was to retrieve it and deliver it to Darrett when the danger had passed. The request was quiet but heavy. Darrett had grown in courage, but Becklin knew war did not always spare those who still had lessons to teach.

By sunset, Vogler stood between surrender and slaughter. The Red Army was coming, the boats were not yet fully away, and the party remained behind with the task of seeing the people through.
