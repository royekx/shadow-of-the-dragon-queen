Unexpected Journey 008

Ashes on the River

The river carries ghosts. Kalaman rises ahead, but the dead walk the waters.

← Previous Brief Account Next →
UJ 008 Party Level 4 Vingaard River → Kalaman

The Vingaard River carried the survivors away from Vogler, but it could not carry them away from what they had seen. Behind them, smoke swallowed the town that had welcomed them in Ispin's name. Ahead waited Kalaman, half a day downriver, and the uncertain mercy of walls, soldiers, and councils. Between those two places, the boats drifted through grief.

Roshi found Darrett Highwater shaken by the weight of all that had happened. Becklin Uth Viharin was gone, Vogler was burning behind them, and the young squire carried the look of someone who had survived when others had not. Roshi presented him with Becklin's armor, but Darrett could not bring himself to accept it at first. He thanked her, yet said he did not feel worthy to wear it. He had not done enough, he believed. He had not saved Vogler. Nearby, Bakaris and his son gave voice to the same cruel thought, suggesting more might have been done to save the village. The words settled poorly among the party, feeding a resentment that had already begun to harden around both men.

During the short rest that followed, the relics pulled from the ruins of battle found new hands. Kakashi attuned himself to the Boilerdrake Heart, the hot gem taken from the wreckage of the mechanical dragon. Aratos Stormblessed took up the Emberwake Greataxe, its heat and promise waiting beneath the surface. The river kept moving, and for a little while, exhaustion gave the survivors silence.

Then the dead appeared in the water.

At first, the villagers saw figures in the river and mistook them for people in need of rescue. Hands reached over the sides of boats. Voices cried out. But those who tried to help were pulled beneath the surface. The river became a mirror for grief, showing each person something different, something sharp enough to draw them close.

Darrett saw Becklin Uth Viharin and was dragged into the water. Jasper Quickwit saw Lucky Leppomanto, who warned him that time was running out on the task he had been given. Aratos saw two knights: Cathan Twice-Born and Lord Soth, standing as opposing shadows of legacy and choice. They told him he would have to choose soon, and Aratos held himself against the pull. Mayariel saw the Dragon Queen, who promised that her sword would find her, and the vision drew her down. Roshi saw Ispin Greenshield questioning whether she had truly protected the villagers. Inwardly, Roshi answered that she had done what she could, and that truth kept her from surrendering to the river.

The apparitions rose as Ashen Vigils, burning with violet and purple flame upon the water. The party fought them back while the boats rocked with fear and the villagers cried out around them. Steel, spell, and stubborn will broke the dead things apart, and those taken by the water were pulled back from the brink. Once again, the party saved the people of Vogler, though the victory carried no triumph. Somewhere beyond mortal sight, the encounter stirred a distant awareness. Lord Soth had brushed against their path, and though he did not yet move against them, the shadow of his attention now lingered.

When Kalaman finally rose ahead, Mayor Raven Uth Vogler gathered herself for the sake of her people. She ensured the refugees were seen to and asked the party to speak before the council on Vogler's behalf. Darrett was asked to accompany them. Before they entered the city, the party returned to the matter of Becklin's armor. They encouraged Darrett to wear it, not because he had already become what Becklin was, but because his people needed him to stand. This time, Darrett accepted. He donned the armor, not as a claim of worth already proven, but as a promise to grow into the burden placed upon him.

At the gates, Bakaris and his son were nowhere to be found. Darrett addressed the guards and told them Vogler had fallen. The guards revealed that Bakaris had already entered the city to speak with the council, and the party was escorted toward the council chambers. There, Bakaris the Younger stood in their way with all the arrogance his bloodline could lend him. He claimed his father would handle the matter and dismissed the refugees as "fishfolk." The insult struck hard. Kakashi cut into both father and son with open disdain, while Roshi and Aratos made it clear that the boy did not speak for them. At last, they were allowed inside.

The council chamber held Governor Miat, Marshal Vendri, Lord Bakaris, Darrett Highwater, and the party. Governor Miat confirmed that Bakaris had already brought news of Vogler's fall, then asked whether the people of Vogler intended retaliation. Darrett answered with clarity: they sought refuge, not war. Bakaris tried to twist and undermine the discussion, but Aratos silenced him physically before his words could poison the room further. The party offered their service in Kalaman's military efforts in exchange for aid to the displaced villagers. Governor Miat warned that Vogler was not alone; other villages had come under attack as well. Marshal Vendri supported the claim, making clear that the region itself was under pressure.

While the council deliberated, Kakashi attempted to send his magical raven back into the chamber. A guard struck it down, causing it to vanish before it could draw too much suspicion. Aratos's distraction helped keep the raven's true nature from becoming the focus of the moment. Jasper, meanwhile, listened where others could not. He overheard the council's private discussion and learned that Governor Miat held firm control despite Bakaris's interruptions. Marshal Vendri also spoke of Fizban and Aeimos aiding other villages, a sign that the party's allies had not vanished from the wider struggle.

Outside, Aratos examined statues in the courtyard with Squire Roberto. Among them stood figures once honored by the Knights of Solamnia, including Lord Soth. The memory of Soth's story surfaced: once celebrated, then fallen through selfishness and personal failure, now isolated in disgrace and dread. Aratos's interest sharpened around the figure, especially after the river vision. Roberto suggested Marshal Vendri as the one most worth speaking to about such matters.

Jasper noticed something else as the council members exited. One figure shimmered faintly with illusion, and Jasper recognized the sign of the Dragon's Eye. The acknowledgment between them was subtle, quiet, and dangerous. No words were exchanged, but none were needed. The guild's reach had found Kalaman too.

When the council returned with its decision, Governor Miat granted refuge to the people of Vogler. The condition was clear: the party would aid Kalaman's military efforts. The group hesitated at the idea of formal conscription, but Darrett stepped forward and agreed to serve as intermediary. His choice gave shape to the agreement and kept the party from being bound too tightly while still honoring the promise made for Vogler's people. Aratos identified himself as a Knight Radiant, and Marshal Vendri acknowledged the title. Further missions would follow, and Vendri requested a name for the party so the city could record who had entered its service.

Afterward, Kakashi and Jasper went to Wyhan's Apothecary and presented their High Sorcery scrolls. Wyhan examined their paths and determined that both would test for the White Robes. The test would not come immediately; a practical exam was scheduled several weeks out. Once business was done, Wyhan dismissed them with little ceremony, leaving the two mages with another road ahead of them inside the larger storm.

Mayariel, meanwhile, noticed the absence of the golden finch that had accompanied her. Sunscale returned to Fizban, who greeted the party warmly and with sadness for Vogler. He spoke of pending matters before his own departure, but Sunscale made its choice clear. The golden finch remained with Mayariel.

At last, the party returned to Mayor Raven and the refugees. They told her that Kalaman had granted aid, though not freely and not without expectation. Raven received the news with gratitude. Her people had survived the fall of Vogler, crossed the river of ashes, and reached the shelter of Kalaman's walls. The party had become their voice, their shield, and now their bond to the city's military efforts.

The road from Vogler had ended, but the war had not.
