After the Battle
  • The aftermath is quiet the way only a battlefield can be. Bodies lie scattered across High Hill.
  • Becklin gives orders. Cudgel's fury burns hot — she wants answers from a captured traitor.
  • Becklin keeps her composure. Anger cannot make the next decision.
  • Festival banners still hang across Vogler, cheerful and bright. Bloodied survivors are carried beneath them. The contrast is cruel.
The Traitor
  • The captured mercenary is questioned. Cudgel nearly loses herself to rage. Mayor Raven steps in before interrogation becomes vengeance.
  • Aratos leans close and makes the threat clear in a quieter, more dangerous way.
  • The man breaks. He does not know everything — only that Gragonis, Cudgel's lieutenant, went into the woods and returned with gold.
  • Some of the mercenaries were paid to betray. The coin was good. For men with weak loyalty, that was enough.
The Scout
  • The party scouts the woods and finds the Red Army: a force of 100-200 soldiers, positioned to the northwest.
  • Gragonis was indeed paid to betray.
  • Jenkins, a weathered old man living in the woods, approaches. He has supplies — potions, rope, leeching bolts. He gives them to the party.
  • "Vogler helped me once. Now I help Vogler."
Preparation
  • Back in town: Mayor Raven resists the thought of abandoning Vogler. Vogler is home.
  • The party presses the need for a plan beyond pride. Becklin quietly agrees: escape must be prepared.
  • The river and boats offer a possible way out, though vessels will need repairs. Evacuation will be chaotic.
  • Time is running out. The Red Army waits. And the party knows now: this is bigger than one town.
